# Note-Taking Systems

A note-taking system is not a tool — it is a *contract* with your future self about how you will retrieve information you have written down. Three contracts dominate the field.

## Zettelkasten

Atomic notes (one idea each), connected by explicit links. Originally Niklas Luhmann's system on paper index cards; now most often run in Obsidian, Logseq, or Roam. Strength: emergent structure through linking. Weakness: high friction at capture.

## PARA

Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives. A folder hierarchy that mirrors what you are actively working on. Designed by Tiago Forte. Strength: matches how most office work is structured. Weakness: re-organizing is tedious as projects shift.

## Building a Second Brain

CODE: Capture, Organize, Distill, Express. A workflow rather than a structure. Notes flow from capture through to creative output. Strength: opinionated, practical. Weakness: assumes a particular creative-output goal.

## Choosing

Most people pick one and find it doesn't quite fit. The honest answer is to start with whichever feels lowest-friction, and let the actual texture of your work shape the system over time. The best system is the one you actually use.
