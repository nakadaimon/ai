# Kneading Techniques

Traditional kneading was about brute mechanical force — work the dough until it's smooth. Modern home baking has shifted toward gentler methods because they suit higher-hydration doughs better.

## Stretch and fold

Every 30 minutes during bulk ferment, lift one edge of the dough, stretch it up, and fold it over. Rotate the bowl, repeat for all four sides. Three to four sets is typical.

## Slap and fold (Bertinet)

For very wet doughs, use the slap-and-fold method: pick up the dough, slap it on the counter, fold it back on itself, repeat. Builds gluten in 5–10 minutes without flour on the counter.

## When to stop

Test for gluten development with the windowpane test — stretch a small piece thin enough to see light through. If it tears immediately, more time or another fold is needed.
