# Sourdough Basics

Sourdough is the oldest form of leavened bread — yeast and lactobacilli capture themselves out of the air and live in a starter culture you keep going indefinitely.

## Starter

A starter is flour and water in roughly 1:1 ratio by weight. Feed daily at room temperature, or weekly if refrigerated. A healthy starter doubles within 4–8 hours of feeding.

## Hydration

Hydration is the ratio of water to flour, expressed as a percentage. 65% is firm and easy to handle; 80% is wet and produces an open crumb. Beginners usually start at 70%.

## Fermentation

Fermentation has two phases: bulk ferment (4–6h at 24°C) and final proof (1–2h, or overnight in the fridge). Cold proofing develops flavour and makes scoring easier.

## Common mistakes

Most failures trace back to three causes: starter not active enough, dough underproofed, oven too cold. Diagnose by tracking volume change during bulk ferment.
